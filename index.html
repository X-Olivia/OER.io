Menstrual Cycle Prediction & Inference

An open educational resource for existing work, taxonomies, datasets, code, and research outputs in menstrual prediction and cycle inference—situated in the context of embodied care and sparse physiological sensing.

What this OER contains

Field survey

Background, problem definition, physiological constraints (ovulation as anchor; ovulatory vs anovulatory cycles), physiological correlates of ovulation from wearables, and a taxonomy of computational approaches with reported performance limits and research directions.

Taxonomies

Menstrual type classification (e.g. ovulatory vs anovulatory; regular vs irregular) and research method classification (calendar-based, physiology-based; data-driven vs physiology-informed signal processing).

Datasets & code

Open datasets (e.g. mcPHASES on PhysioNet, Kaggle menstrual cycle data) and data-processing code for hormonal alignment, ovulation annotation, and day-level feature construction.

Research & paper

Interim report on a physiology-informed, data-driven MC and ovulation inference framework for sparse sensing, plus embodied menstrual service robot integration; baseline reproduction results and implementation notes.
