About

What this OER is trying to do, and how to read it.

Purpose

This site is an open educational resource for menstrual cycle prediction research. It is written to help new researchers enter the area without immediately getting lost in inconsistent terminology, mixed evaluation protocols, or the false simplicity of the 28-day cycle template.

The current version is aimed at three audiences: newcomers who need a map of the field, researchers who want a clearer overview of available data and open problems, and collaborators who need a concise explanation of the present project direction.

Editorial principles

Newcomer-first structure

The site explains the biology, then the method families, then the dataset and evaluation issues. That order is deliberate.

Primary-source citations

Where possible, pages point to primary papers, clinical guidance, and dataset pages rather than summary posts.

Distinguish evidence routes

Calendar, wearable, and clinical approaches are kept separate so their assumptions and tradeoffs stay visible.

Be explicit about uncertainty

The site intentionally highlights irregular-cycle difficulty, label ambiguity, and the limits of public data access.

Author / project context

This OER accompanies the research project on menstrual cycle prediction from wearable physiological data, with a particular interest in irregular-cycle analysis, personalisation, and practical support for future embodied or assistive systems.

The site content is currently in English to match the underlying report and research literature. A bilingual version could be added later if public-facing outreach becomes a priority.
