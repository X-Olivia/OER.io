Literature Deep Dive

A verified reading list for menstrual cycle prediction. Every item below was checked against two independent traces when possible: a DOI or publisher page, and PubMed or PMC indexing.

Foundations Wearable studies Recent 2025-2026 Methods and reviews

Foundational cycle timing, apps, and self-tracking

Year Paper Focus Why it matters Link
1997 Martinez AR. Prediction and detection of the fertile phase of the menstrual cycle: an overview. Classical overview of fertile-phase prediction. Useful baseline for understanding how much current work still inherits older fertility-awareness assumptions. DOI PubMed
2000 Wilcox AJ, Dunson D, Baird DD. The timing of the fertile window in the menstrual cycle. Prospective fertility-window timing study. Foundational evidence that ovulation timing varies widely and is not pinned to cycle midpoint. DOI PubMed
2016 Setton R, Tierney C, Tsai T. The Accuracy of Web Sites and Cellular Phone Applications in Predicting the Fertile Window. Consumer tool evaluation. Shows why app predictions must be interpreted cautiously. DOI PubMed
2016 Moglia ML, Nguyen HV, Chyjek K, et al. Evaluation of Smartphone Menstrual Cycle Tracking Applications. App quality evaluation. Helpful for understanding the gap between user-facing product claims and scientific evidence. DOI PubMed
2016 Li D, Heyer L, Jennings VH. Personalised estimation of a woman's most fertile days. Personalised fertile-window estimation from calendar-style records. An early example of moving beyond fixed calendar heuristics toward user-specific inference. DOI PubMed
2019 Symul L, Wac K, Hillard PJA, Salathé M. Assessment of menstrual health status and evolution through mobile apps for fertility awareness. App-based population analysis. Important for large-scale self-tracking evidence and for showing phase variability. DOI PubMed
2019 Bull JR, Rowland SP, Berglund Scherwitzl E, et al. Real-world menstrual cycle characteristics of more than 600,000 menstrual cycles. Large-scale real-world cycle variability. One of the clearest papers showing that the textbook 28-day norm is not enough. DOI PubMed
2020 Grieger JA, Norman RJ. Menstrual Cycle Length and Patterns in a Global Cohort of Women Using a Mobile Phone App. Global app cohort study. Useful for cross-population cycle-length distribution and app-based cohort interpretation. DOI PubMed
2020 Li K, Urteaga I, Wiggins CH, et al. Characterizing physiological and symptomatic variation in menstrual cycles using self-tracked mobile-health data. Large self-tracked cohort analysis. Important for symptom variability and cycle-structure heterogeneity. DOI PubMed
2021 Li K, Urteaga I, Wiggins CH, Elhadad N. A predictive model for next cycle start date that accounts for adherence in menstrual self-tracking. Prediction with adherence-aware missingness modelling. Strong reminder that user behaviour and biological behaviour should not be conflated. DOI PubMed
2022 Symul L, Holmes S. Labeling Self-Tracked Menstrual Health Records With Hidden Semi-Markov Models. HSMM labelling of self-tracked records. Useful for understanding latent-state modelling in noisy app data. DOI PubMed

Wearable sensing, passive physiology, and prediction

Year Paper Signals / task Why it matters Link
2017 Shilaih M, Goodale BM, Falco L, et al. Pulse Rate Measurement During Sleep Using Wearable Sensors. Sleeping pulse rate across cycle phases. Early evidence that cardiac signals track cycle phase in free-living conditions. DOI PubMed
2017 Leiva RA, Bouchard TP, Abdullah SH, et al. Urinary Luteinizing Hormone Tests: Which Concentration Threshold Best Predicts Ovulation? LH thresholding for ovulation prediction. Important for reading wearable papers that treat urinary LH as "ground truth" without questioning the operational threshold. DOI PMC
2018 Shilaih M, Degroote L, Falco L, et al. Modern fertility awareness methods: wrist wearables capture temperature changes associated with the menstrual cycle. Wrist temperature and fertility-awareness relevance. Directly relevant to why wrist wearables are now taken seriously in the field. DOI PubMed
2018 Regidor PA, Kaczmarczyk M, Schiweck E, et al. Identification and prediction of the fertile window with a vaginal biosensor for measuring circadian and circamensual core body temperature. Vaginal core-temperature biosensor. Useful contrast case showing what higher-fidelity temperature sensing can achieve compared with wrist or finger skin temperature. DOI PubMed
2019 Goodale BM, Shilaih M, Falco L, et al. Wearable Sensors Reveal Menses-Driven Changes in Physiology and Enable Prediction of the Fertile Window. Temperature, HRV, and multimodal fertile-window estimation. A central wearable paper for multimodal passive sensing. DOI PubMed
2019 Maijala A, Kinnunen H, Koskimäki H, et al. Nocturnal finger skin temperature in menstrual cycle tracking. Ring-based finger temperature for ovulation and menstruation detection. One of the most cited free-living distal-temperature studies. DOI PubMed
2020 Grant AD, Newman M, Kriegsfeld LJ. Ultradian rhythms in heart rate variability and distal body temperature anticipate onset of the luteinizing hormone surge. HRV plus distal temperature for LH-surge anticipation. Important because it argues that sub-daily rhythmic structure contains useful signal before the ovulatory event. DOI PubMed
2020 Luo L, She X, Cao J, et al. Detection and Prediction of Ovulation From Body Temperature Measured by an In-Ear Wearable Thermometer. In-ear wearable thermometer plus HMM-style modelling. Shows that higher-frequency temperature sensing can support both detection and prediction workflows. DOI PubMed
2021 Zhu TY, Rothenbühler M, Hamvas G, et al. The Accuracy of Wrist Skin Temperature in Detecting Ovulation Compared to Basal Body Temperature. Wrist temperature versus BBT. Useful for comparing consumer wearable signals against the traditional BBT reference. DOI PubMed
2022 Yu JL, Su YF, Zhang C, et al. Tracking of menstrual cycles and prediction of the fertile window via measurements of basal body temperature and heart rate as well as machine-learning algorithms. BBT plus wearable HR with machine-learning prediction, including irregular users. Directly relevant to your stated interest in irregular populations and multimodal forecasting. DOI Publisher
2023 Sides K, Kilungeja G, Tapia M, et al. Analyzing physiological signals recorded with a wearable sensor across the menstrual cycle using circular statistics. Temperature, HR, IBI, EDA, and circular-statistics analysis. Useful because it frames the cycle as a periodic structure rather than a standard linear sequence problem. DOI PubMed
2023 Hamidovic A, Davis J, Wardle M, et al. Periovulatory Subphase of the Menstrual Cycle Is Marked by a Significant Decrease in Heart Rate Variability. HRV decrease around the periovulatory subphase. Helpful for interpreting what part of the cycle cardiac-autonomic features may be most informative for. DOI PubMed
2023 Niggli A, Rothenbühler M, Sachs M, Leeners B. Can Wrist-Worn Medical Devices Correctly Identify Ovulation? Prospective and retrospective wrist-worn ovulation identification. Useful recent comparison of realistic wrist-worn device performance against urinary ovulation tests. DOI PubMed
2024 Gombert-Labedens M, Alzueta E, Perez-Amparan E, et al. Using wearable skin temperature data to advance tracking and characterization of the menstrual cycle in a real-world setting. High-resolution wearable skin-temperature characterization in real-world conditions. Useful for moving beyond simple biphasic assumptions toward richer rhythm characterization. DOI PubMed
2024 Alzueta E, Gombert-Labedens M, Javitz H, et al. Menstrual Cycle Variations in Wearable-Detected Finger Temperature and Heart Rate, But Not in Sleep Metrics, in Young and Midlife Individuals. Finger temperature, HR, sleep, and age-stratified menstrual variation. Useful because it directly separates signals that vary robustly across the cycle from those that do not. DOI PubMed
2024 Lin G, Li JY, Christofferson K, et al. Understanding wrist skin temperature changes to hormone variations across the menstrual cycle. Wrist skin temperature linked to E3G and LH variation. Especially useful because it directly examines the relation between wearable temperature and hormone changes rather than only prediction accuracy. DOI PubMed
2025 Wang Y, et al. Performance of algorithms using wrist temperature for retrospective ovulation day estimate and next menses start day prediction. Apple wrist-temperature algorithms. Large, recent prospective evidence on wrist-temperature algorithm performance. DOI PubMed
2025 Thigpen N, Patel S, Zhang X. Oura Ring as a Tool for Ovulation Detection: Validation Analysis. Commercial ring validation for ovulation detection. Useful because it directly benchmarks a widely used consumer ring against calendar methods and subgroup conditions. DOI PubMed
2025 Masuda H, Okada S, Shiozawa N, et al. Machine learning model for menstrual cycle phase classification and ovulation day detection based on sleeping heart rate under free-living conditions. Sleeping heart rate and ML. A strong recent example of cardiac-first modelling rather than temperature-first modelling. DOI PubMed
2025 Luo C, Su YF, Ren YY, et al. Prediction of the fertile window and menstruation with a wearable device via machine-learning algorithms. Wearable ML prediction for fertile window and menstruation. Relevant because it explicitly includes both regular and irregular menstruators using wrist temperature, HRV, HR, and respiratory rate. DOI PubMed
2025 Kilungeja G, Graham K, Liu X, Nasseri M. Machine learning-based menstrual phase identification using wearable device data. Passive wearable phase identification using ML. Important because it tackles phase identification from wrist-device data without relying on daily user input. DOI Publisher

Recent 2025-2026 papers

Year Paper Focus Why it matters Link
2025 Kim R, Kim YK, Song CB, et al. Consistent Ovulation Window Prediction based on Physiological Temporal Variability Patterns from Wearable Devices. Wearable time-variability patterns for ovulation-window prediction. Useful as a recent engineering-style paper focused on temporal variability rather than only static summary features. DOI PubMed
2025 Luo C, Su YF, Ren YY, et al. Prediction of the fertile window and menstruation with a wearable device via machine-learning algorithms. Prospective wearable ML for fertile-window and menses prediction. One of the clearest recent studies that explicitly reports performance for both regular and irregular menstruators. DOI PubMed
2025 Wang Y, et al. Performance of algorithms using wrist temperature for retrospective ovulation day estimate and next menses start day prediction. Wrist-temperature algorithms for ovulation and menses timing. Large, recent evidence on retrospective ovulation-day estimation and next-menses prediction from wrist temperature. DOI PubMed
2025 Masuda H, Okada S, Shiozawa N, et al. Machine learning model for menstrual cycle phase classification and ovulation day detection based on sleeping heart rate under free-living conditions. Sleeping HR for phase classification and ovulation-day detection. A strong recent cardiac-first alternative to temperature-dominant approaches. DOI PubMed
2026 Shi Y, Wang CC, Yang Y, et al. The diagnostic accuracy of wearable digital technology in detecting fertility window and menstrual cycles: a systematic review and Bayesian network meta-analysis. Systematic review and Bayesian network meta-analysis of wearable diagnostic accuracy. Important because it synthesizes recent evidence and makes cross-device comparison more explicit. DOI PubMed
2026 de Jager E, Caulfield B, Angelidi E, et al. Wearable-Derived Heart Rate Variability Across the Menstrual Cycle, Hormonal Contraceptive Use, and Reproductive Life Stages in Females: A Living Systematic Review. Living systematic review of wearable-derived HRV. Useful if you want a more up-to-date interpretation of how robust HRV findings really are. DOI PubMed
2026 Lin G, Li JY, Kalani K, Truong KN, Mariakakis A. A longitudinal dataset of physiological, hormonal, metabolic, and self-reported menstrual health data. Extended dataset paper for mcPHASES-style multimodal menstrual health data. Relevant because it strengthens the open-data side of the field in 2026, including metabolic data and structured multimodal records. DOI PubMed
2026 Gombert Labedens M, Baker FC. The menstrual clock, an open access tool to identify and illustrate menstrual phases, windows, and events. Open-access tool for menstrual phase and event identification. Useful for standardizing how phases and windows are operationalized and communicated. DOI PubMed
2026 Milad HC, Cromack SC, Walter JR. Menstrual tracking technologies and fertility: evaluating accuracy, utility, and impact on time to pregnancy. Clinical review of tracking technologies and fertility outcomes. Good recent bridge between device evaluation and fertility-clinic relevance. DOI PubMed

Methods papers, datasets, and reviews

Year Paper Focus Why it matters Link
2020 Baker FC, Siboza F, Fuller A. Temperature regulation in women: Effects of the menstrual cycle. Thermoregulation review. Useful background for interpreting why temperature is informative yet not trivial. DOI PubMed
2025 Lin B, Li JY, Kalani K, Truong K, Mariakakis A. mcPHASES: A Dataset of Physiological, Hormonal, and Self-reported Events and Symptoms for Menstrual Health Tracking with Wearables. Open dataset release. One of the most useful open resources for verified wearable-plus-hormone work. DOI PhysioNet
2024 Lyzwinski L, Elgendi M, Menon C. Innovative Approaches to Menstruation and Fertility Tracking Using Wearable Reproductive Health Technology: Systematic Review. Systematic review of wearable reproductive-health technologies. Useful broad review if you want an overview of device categories, sensing modalities, and current application areas. DOI PubMed
2024 Cromack SC, Walter JR. Consumer wearables and personal devices for tracking the fertile window. Narrative review of the consumer-device landscape. Useful for understanding which tools are commercially available and how they are positioned for fertility tracking. DOI PubMed
2024 Balen AH, Tamblyn J, Skorupskaite K, Munro MG. A comprehensive review of the new FIGO classification of ovulatory disorders. Ovulatory-disorder review. Important for not treating irregular cycling as a single homogeneous state. DOI PubMed
2025 Khairunisa M, Putri DMSA, Wijayakusuma IGNL. Comparison of Machine Learning Methods for Menstrual Cycle Analysis and Prediction. Comparative ML benchmark paper. Useful as a compact baseline-comparison paper, though methodologically simpler than the strongest physiology-aware studies. DOI Publisher
2020 Giersch GEW, Morrissey MC, Katch RK, et al. Menstrual cycle and thermoregulation during exercise in the heat: A systematic review and meta-analysis. Thermoregulation meta-analysis. Useful background for interpreting thermal physiology rather than using it as a black-box signal. DOI PubMed

This page intentionally mixes prediction papers, signal-validation papers, app-scale epidemiology, and reviews. A researcher entering the field usually needs all four, because prediction claims only make sense when the physiology, the label source, and the evaluation contract are all visible.
